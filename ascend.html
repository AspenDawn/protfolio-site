Project Overview

Ascend is a budgeting app that aims to encourage and help users manage their funds to successfully reach their short term financial goals.

The Problem

Many people find finances to be intimidating and an overwhelming experience all together. This often makes saving up a difficult task for most people and result in them either not making their goals in their desired timeframe or giving up on them entirely.

The Solution

To enable the user to take control of their finances and attain their goals. Ascend is there to keep track of purchase, spending and their goal deadlines. The app also provides the user with simple solutions based on quick analysis of the users spending habits.

Useability Test

Users were asked to login to the app, and track a purchase that they had just made.

Key Insights

  • All Users were able to successfully navigate the app and track a purchase.
  • The track expense screen needs an input section to label their expense.
  • drop down section for already pre-established categories.
  • The goals icon needed to change for better communication.
  • A percentage in the middle of the goal's progress circle for a more accurate read about their progress.
  • Some of the bottom navigation icons were a bit abstract so labels were noted to be helpful.
  • LIked that the app language was positive and focused on saving as opposed to spending.

Next Steps

  • More view options for charts.
  • Expand into long term financial goals.
  • Link Bank accounts and credit cards.
  • Manage more than 3 goals

Project Reflection

  • Making sure that all information displayed is relevant and easy to understand for the user's goals but also presenting it in a way that makes the user want to interact with the UI.
  • Developing a clear hierarchy of information that depicts what needs to be presented at a glance on the dashboard and what can be displayed on a more detailed screen.
  • Taking into consideration “power users” and what aspects may become bothersome to them.
